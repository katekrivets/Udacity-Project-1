OWL

Owls are almost always nocturnal which means they will only be active at night. It is very rare that you will see one during the day.

  • Order: Strigiformes
  • Smallest owl: 13.5 cm
  • Largest owl: 71 cm
  • Habitat: Everywhere in the world, except Antarctica

Owls are birds from the order Strigifor- mes, which includes about 200 species of mostly solitary and nocturnal birds of prey typified by an upright stance, a large, broad head, binocular vision, binaural hearing, sharp talons, and feathers adapted for silent flight.
